Insertion Sort

Definition

Insertion Sort is a simple sorting algorithm that builds the final sorted array one item at a time.
It works by taking elements from the unsorted portion and inserting them at their correct position in the sorted portion.

Pseudocode

for i = 1 to n-1:
    key = arr[i]
    j = i - 1
    while j >= 0 and arr[j] > key:
        arr[j + 1] = arr[j]
        j = j - 1
    arr[j + 1] = key

Time & Space Complexity

  • Best Case: O(n) - already sorted
  • Average Case: O(n²)
  • Worst Case: O(n²)
  • Space: O(1) - in-place sorting

Real-World Applications

  • Efficient for small datasets or partially sorted data.
  • Common in simple text editors for sorting names or numbers.
  • Used in online insertion or real-time sorting.

Real-Life Analogy

Think of arranging playing cards in your hand. You pick one card at a time and place it in the correct position among the already sorted cards. That's Insertion Sort in action!
